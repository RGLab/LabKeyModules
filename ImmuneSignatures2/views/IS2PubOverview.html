Principal Investigator: Steven Kleinstein
Title: Immune Signatures II
Number of participants: 1721
Brief description: Identification of vaccine response signatures in order to determine common signatures associated with successful responses across different vaccines
Description: show

A central goal of the Human Immunology Project Consortium (HIPC) is the identification of molecular “signatures” that correlate with the outcomes of vaccinations, adjuvants or natural infections in humans. These signature result from the computational analysis of large-scale experimental data from high-throughput systems biology approaches coupled with detailed clinical phenotyping in well-characterized human cohorts.

This IS2 study presents results related to one of our motivating biological projects where we have focused on identifying vaccination response signatures in young adults and a comparative meta-analysis across different vaccines. We sought to determine whether there was a common signature associated with successful responses across different vaccines.

Objectives: show

The goal of the highly collaborative “signatures project” proposed is identifying, standardizing and disseminating HIPC immune signatures.

Endpoints: show
  1. Develop a data model and submission system to standardize immune signatures involving genes, proteins, cell subsets and metabolites, as well as a dashboard to disseminate this information
  2. Populate the data model by manually curating signatures from HIPC and related publications, as well as generating new signatures through a re-analysis of existing data
  3. Carry out motivating biological projects
Available datasets: Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA), Hemagglutination inhibition (HAI), Neutralizing antibody titer
Available raw data files: Gene expression microarray data files
