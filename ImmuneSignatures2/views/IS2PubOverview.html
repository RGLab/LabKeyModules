Principal Investigator: Steven H. Kleinstein
Title: HIPC-II Immune Signatures Data Resource and Analysis (IS2)
Brief description: The HIPC Immune Signatures Data Resource provides integrated host response data from human vaccination studies involving multiple vaccines. Pre-vaccination and temporal response profiles are analyzed to identify unique and common signatures, including those associated with differential antibody responses.
Description: show

A central goal of the Human Immunology Project Consortium (HIPC) is the identification of molecular “signatures” that correlate with the outcomes of vaccinations, adjuvants or natural infections in humans. These signatures result from the computational analysis of large-scale experimental data from high-throughput systems biology approaches coupled with detailed clinical phenotyping in well-characterized human cohorts.

As part of the highly collaborative HIPC-II “Signatures Project,” we have created the Immune Signatures Data Resource, a compendium of standardized systems vaccinology datasets. This data resource includes code to reproduce the processing and batch normalization starting from the underlying study data in ImmPort and the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO). In addition, we provide codes to analyze these data to identify unique and common signatures, including those associated with differential antibody responses.

Objectives: show
  1. Create a compendium of standardized systems vaccinology datasets to support comparative analyses.
  2. Identify a common pre-vaccination signature and mechanisms that predict the immune response across a wide variety of vaccines.
  3. Identify common signatures and mechanisms associated with the temporal response or predictive of vaccine immunogenicity.
